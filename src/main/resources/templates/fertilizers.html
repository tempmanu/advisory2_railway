Fertilizer Guide

Common fertilizers & application tips

🌿 DAP — Di-Ammonium Phosphate

A widely-used phosphorus fertilizer with 18% Nitrogen and 46% P₂O₅. Apply as a basal dose before sowing for best results.

📦 Composition: 18-46-0 (N-P-K)
⏱️ Application: Basal dose before sowing / transplanting
🌾 Best for: Rice, Wheat, Cotton, Maize
📏 Dosage: 40–50 kg/acre depending on crop

🔵 Urea (यूरिया)

The most common nitrogen fertilizer. Contains 46% Nitrogen. Always apply in splits to prevent nutrient loss and burning.

📦 Composition: 46% Nitrogen
⏱️ Application: Split doses — basal + top dressing
🌾 Best for: All crops — primary nitrogen source
⚠️ Caution: Avoid over-application — leads to lodging

🟡 MOP — Muriate of Potash

Main potassium fertilizer. Contains 60% K₂O. Improves drought resistance, fruit quality, and disease tolerance in crops.

📦 Composition: 0-0-60 (K₂O)
⏱️ Application: Basal + split as top dressing
🌾 Best for: Cotton, Fruits, Vegetables
📏 Dosage: 20–30 kg/acre

🔴 Zinc Sulphate (జింక్ సల్ఫేట్)

Micronutrient fertilizer. Zinc deficiency is very common in Telangana soils, especially rice-growing areas. Spray or soil-apply for correction.

📦 Contains: 21% or 33% Zinc
⏱️ Application: Soil — 5 kg/acre; Foliar — 0.5% solution
🌾 Best for: Rice, Cotton — zinc-deficient soils
💡 Signs of deficiency: Yellowing between leaf veins

🟢 Lime / Agricultural Lime

Used to correct acidic soils (pH below 6.0). Calcium carbonate raises soil pH and improves nutrient availability.

📦 Contains: Calcium Carbonate (CaCO₃)
⏱️ Application: 2–3 weeks before sowing
📏 Dosage: 200–400 kg/acre for acidic soils
💡 Use when soil pH is below 6.0
