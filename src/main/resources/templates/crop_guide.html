Crop Guide

Sowing seasons & best practices for Telangana

🌾 Rice (వరి)

Kharif crop — sown June to July, harvested October to November. Requires clayey or loamy soil with good water retention. Ideal pH: 5.5–7.0.

🗓️ Sowing season: June – July (Kharif)
💧 Water requirement: High — needs standing water during tillering
🌡️ Temperature: 20°C – 38°C optimal
🏆 Best varieties: Sona Masuri, BPT-5204, MTU-1010

🌿 Cotton (పత్తి)

Kharif crop suited for black soil in Telangana. Sow May to June and harvest October to January. Requires deep, well-drained black cotton soil.

🗓️ Sowing season: May – June (Kharif)
💧 Water requirement: Medium — drought-tolerant once established
🌡️ Temperature: 21°C – 35°C optimal
🏆 Best varieties: Bt Cotton hybrids, NHH-44

🌽 Maize (మొక్కజొన్న)

Both Kharif and Rabi crop. Well-drained loamy soil preferred. Grows well in Telangana's climate. Short duration of 90–120 days.

🗓️ Sowing season: June – July & Oct – Nov
💧 Water requirement: Medium — sensitive to waterlogging
🌡️ Temperature: 18°C – 32°C optimal
🏆 Best varieties: DEKALB 9141, Pioneer 3396

🌾 Wheat (గోధుమ)

Rabi crop grown in cooler months. Suited for loamy to clay-loam soils. Ideal for irrigated conditions in Telangana's northern districts.

🗓️ Sowing season: November – December (Rabi)
💧 Water requirement: 4–5 irrigations during season
🌡️ Temperature: 10°C – 25°C optimal
🏆 Best varieties: HD-2781, GW-322, K-307
