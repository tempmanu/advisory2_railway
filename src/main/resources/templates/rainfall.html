📊 Annual Rainfall Overview

Telangana receives average annual rainfall of 900–1100 mm. About 75% of this falls during the Southwest Monsoon (June–September).

🌦️ Pre-Monsoon (Mar–May): ~50–80 mm
🌧️ SW Monsoon (Jun–Sep): ~700–850 mm (main season)
🌦️ NE Monsoon (Oct–Nov): ~100–150 mm
☀️ Winter/Summer (Dec–Feb): ~20–40 mm

🗺️ Rainfall by Region

Rainfall distribution varies significantly across Telangana's districts. Plan crop selection based on your region's typical rainfall.

🟢 High Rainfall (>1100mm): Adilabad, Khammam, Warangal
🟡 Medium (700–1100mm): Hyderabad, Nalgonda, Medak
🔴 Low (<700mm): Mahabubnagar, Ranga Reddy

🌱 Rainfall-Based Crop Planning

Choose crops matching your area's rainfall to reduce water stress and improve yields without excessive irrigation.

💧 High Rainfall areas: Ideal for Rice, Sugarcane, Banana
🌿 Medium Rainfall: Cotton, Maize, Soybean, Sunflower
🌾 Low Rainfall / Rainfed: Sorghum, Millets, Pulses
💡 For accuracy, use our Soil Analysis tool for recommendations

⚠️ Rainfall Advisory Tips

🌧️ Avoid fertilizer application 24 hrs before expected heavy rain
💊 Do not spray pesticides if rain forecast within 4–6 hours
🚜 Ensure proper field drainage to prevent waterlogging
📦 Store harvested produce before monsoon onset
🌱 Use rain gauges to track actual field-level rainfall
